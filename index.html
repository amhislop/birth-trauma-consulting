Caring for those affected by birth trauma

For clinicians and health providers who want to improve the quality of the lives of women and their partners following traumatic childbirth.

Our Vision

To be the leading postnatal agency in Australia that providesbirth trauma advocacy and access to improved health care by facilitating evidence based management, support and optimal communication for women and partners suffering from the effects of pelvic organ prolapse, bowel, urinary, sexual impairment and postpartum PTSD.

Recent international research observes that more women than previously realized suffer ‘silently’ from physical and psychological effects of vaginal birth damage that they describe as “…hidden and overlooked’.

Since the introduction of imaging in the 21st Century, international studies reveal that during lengthy vaginal births using forceps to deliver large babies, a pelvic floor structure known as the levator ani muscle, becomes detached from the pubic bone. This injury is different to the more well-known perineal damage and can result in the bladder, bowel and/ or uterus prolapsing into the vagina. Other research shows that both types of birth damage have debilitating consequences for women.

Perinatal mental health research reports that vaginal birthinjury symptoms are poorly identified, embarrassing to explain and often result in barriers to care with reduced quality of life, marital harmony and postpartum PTSD. 1,2,3

An expert birth trauma consultant works respectfully with individuals, couples and/or groups of clinicians to identify their gaps in knowledge regarding physical, sexual and psychological effects of vaginal birth trauma, that may have prevented access to postnatal health improvement.

Birth Trauma Consultancy advocates that health literacy strategies, supported by World Health Organization [W.H.0.] directives, are an integral means of disseminating current research findings on poorly understood effects of vaginal birth trauma to facilitate evidence based decisions of health improvement and reduce obstacles to postnatal healthcare.

Sessions provide empathetic insight and support for maternal sexual dysfunction after vaginal birth damage, that is reported by mothers as “…a taboo topic and secret women’s business”.

Programs are based on findings from published interviews with traumatized women and partners and does not seek to apportion blame to health providers.

Birth Trauma Consultancy promotes integration of care between health providers, maternity and mental health clinicians to improve communication and postnatal outcomes for traumatized women and partners.

Birth Trauma Consultancy programs are relevant to:

Perinatal clinicians who:
  • Manage women with major pelvic floor & perineal damage
  • Midwives, obstetricians, gynaecologists, urogynecologists, psychologists & psychiatrists
Postnatal mothers who:
  • Suffer from vaginal birth damage
  • Re-experience traumatic birth events regularly
  • Persistently avoid reminders of birth
  • Experience nightmares, flashbacks, numbness and/or panic attacks
Partners who:
  • Observe women’s emotional trauma symptoms
  • Experience similar symptoms after witnessing births
Couples:
  • Experiencing marital distress related to vaginal birth trauma

Seminars and courses:

1. Provide current research information regarding:

Types of vaginal birth damage; adverse physical and sexual effects; accurate diagnostic imaging assessment & urogynecology specialties.

2. Explain psychological consequences of vaginal birth damage & birth events concerning:

Links between postnatal PTSD & birth damage; access to mental health specialties; assessment instruments & treatment options.

3. Optimize communication between women, partners & clinicians that:

validates vaginal birth trauma; decreases feelings of shame and stigma; assistspartners understand physical, sexual and emotional effects of birth trauma; provides clinicians with current research and diagnostic information.

Research

More women than previously realized report major vaginal birth damage with resultant pelvic organ prolapse, sexual dysfunction, faecal incontinence, linked to postnatal post-traumatic stress disorder [PTSD]. [Skinner et al, 2018]

Currently physical and psychological symptoms of birth trauma are poorly identified and women face barriers to care due to suboptimal information on risk factors of vaginal birth damage. [Skinner et al, 2015; Skinner et al, 2018; Skinner, 2020]

Fathers reveal limited medical understanding of vaginal birth injuries and an inability to reach partners who are emotionally detached. [Skinner, 2020]

Couples report longstanding relationship issues and sexual dysfunction that is poorly communicated to each other and exacerbated by limited medical assessment and validation of birth injuries. [Skinner et al, 2018; Skinner, 2020]

Men are largely unaware of links between vaginal birth injury and maternal PTSD symptoms and, women report abandonment after clinicians misdiagnose them with postnatal depression (PND). [Skinner et al, 2018; Skinner 2020]
